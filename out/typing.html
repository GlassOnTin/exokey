Each pose is derived, not drawn: the fingertip is pushed one switch-travel along that action's own direction, and the joint angles come from the pad Jacobian — the same one the effort model uses. Residual is how much of the required joint torque the muscles fail to produce; 0% means the digit can really do it. The gauntlet is drawn at the radius the physics chose for each strut — thick where the load runs, thin where it does not. Show supports draws every column the printer has to run up from the bed and you then snap off — pillars under the nodes with nothing beneath them, props under the struts too long to bridge. They lie almost flat here because the part prints on its side, so the printer's vertical axis is nearly in the screen plane. Everything else holds itself up.
