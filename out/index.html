The bone. 410 members → 1632 curved sub-beams · 7.54 g beam / 12.7 g hollow tubes (0.8 mm wall) · every surface ≥ 1.5 mm · no spikes, no loose ends, one piece · 481 µm at the wells against a 500 µm gate · 1021 pillars, 0 props. Grown, not re-pruned — the render takes the topology the objective grew bottom-up; a top-down re-prune instead dead-ends in a heavy uniform 34 g membrane — a local optimum it cannot escape (§8.15k).

The sensor. Each well is a contactless-Hall flexure — a magnet on a printed TPU dome over a 3-axis Hall, soft in tilt and in plunge. Every well is five-way on every finger (the ulnar "three-way" limit was a cradle artefact, not muscle). The read-out is now modelled: a keypress swings the Hall ~430 LSB — 200× the sensor noise, the five directions ≥ 78° apart. It prints as a rigid PA frame + a drop-in keyed TPU cradle, and the Hall seats, wire grooves and a wrist nRF52840 housing carve into one watertight solid (§8.15l).

The strap. The band is the convex hull of (skin ∪ device), so it rides over the gauntlet it holds down; a watch-lug captures a pin in shear at each anchor foot; one adjustable strap (21 mm range) fits the 5th–95th percentile population.

The impact. A 50 N knock, not the keypress, is what sizes the structure — it breaks a deflection-only bone (348 MPa vs 70 MPa yield), and it wants a broad, load-sharing skeleton, not the sparse one thickened. Grown with the knock in the loop, co-sized for the gate and the stress, then form-found so its load paths straighten: 23.2 g, 39% lighter than bolting it on (37.7 g, §8.15k).
